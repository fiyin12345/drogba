Didier Drogba

Drogba's Childhood

Didier Drogba was born on the 11th March 1978 in Ivory Coast. He was born in in Abidjan, Ivory Coast and is part of the Bete people. His early childhood was spent in poverty but when he was 5, he was sent to France to live with his uncle Michel Goba. Michel Goba is also a professional football player from Ivory Coast. He started playing for the youth team of USL Dunkerque and started playing professionally for UC Le Mans in 1999.

Drogba's Football Career

Drogba played for Le Mans UC for 4 seasons, but in his second season he played for the B team. Then, he played for Guingamp for 2 season. Then, he played for Marseille for 1 season. After that, he played for Chelsea for 8 seasons. He played his best at Chelsea. Then, he played for SH Shenhua, Galatasaray, Chelsea again, Montreal, Arizona United, and Pheonix Rising before his retirement.

Drogba's Stats

Drogba got 360 goals in 794 career games. He has won 1 Champions Leagues, 4 FA Cups, 4 Premier Leagues, 2 Community Shields, 3 Football League Cups, 1 Turkish League, 1 Turkish Cup, and 1 Turkish Super Cup.
